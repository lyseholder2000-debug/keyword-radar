How KeywordRadar Works

What is this tool?

KeywordRadar monitors the Danish Parliament (Folketinget) in real time and turns raw legislative data into actionable signals for your clients. Instead of reading through hundreds of cases, you see only what matters — scored, explained, and ready to act on.

The process: DATA → SIGNAL → ACTION

Data is pulled from oda.ft.dk (Folketinget's OData API), filtered for recent activity, then scored against each client's relevance profile.

Relevance scoring (4 layers)

Each parliamentary case is scored 0–100 using a layered model. Layers stack — a case matching multiple layers scores higher.

1Direct mentionCompany or brand named explicitly (+60 pts)
2IndustryCore sector keywords: brewery, pharma, wind energy, etc. (up to +40 pts)
3Value chainSupply chain: packaging, agriculture, logistics, energy, retail (up to +25 pts)
4Risk / OpportunityPolicy areas: tax, regulation, sustainability, trade (up to +20 pts)

Score thresholds

High (60+) — Direct client impact. Brief the team immediately.

Medium (25–59) — Industry or supply chain relevant. Flag for next check-in.

Low (1–24) — Peripheral relevance. Log for awareness.

How to read a signal

Click any signal card to open the detail panel with three tabs:

Insight — What happened, why it matters to the selected client, who is affected, and recommended next steps.

Internal Notes — A shared space for Forte Advisory team members to leave observations, context, or questions visible to all advisors working this signal.

Take Action — Generate a draft email or call prep notes for client outreach. Edit, personalize, and send.

Client selector

The row at the top lets you toggle between clients. Each client has its own tailored relevance profile. "All News" shows every case from Folketinget with no scoring — useful for general awareness or spotting new topics.

Where the process starts & ends

Starts: A new case, vote, or proposal is filed in the Danish Parliament. The API updates. KeywordRadar fetches it within minutes.

Ends: An advisor sends a personalized outreach to the right client — backed by context, relevance scoring, and recommended actions. The signal has become a conversation.

Data source

All data comes from oda.ft.dk — the official open data API of the Danish Parliament (Folketinget). Updated continuously as parliamentary proceedings happen.
